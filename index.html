Ancient Monuments

Taj Mahal

A white marble mausoleum in India, built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.

It stands as a symbol of eternal love and attracts millions of tourists.

Eiffel Tower

An iconic iron structure in Paris, France, designed by Gustave Eiffel.

It is a global symbol of romance and one of the most visited landmarks.

Great Wall of China

A vast wall stretching over 13,000 miles, built to protect ancient China.

This UNESCO World Heritage site is a wonder of ancient architecture.

Machu Picchu

An ancient Incan city in the Andes of Peru, known for its historic ruins.

It stands as a symbol of the advanced civilization of the Incas.

The Colosseum

An ancient Roman amphitheater in Italy, used for gladiator combats.

It remains a symbol of Rome's architectural and engineering prowess.

Statue of Liberty

A gift from France to the U.S., symbolizing freedom and democracy.

This iconic statue stands on Liberty Island in New York City.

Stonehenge

A prehistoric monument in England, featuring a ring of massive stones.

Its purpose remains a mystery, attracting visitors worldwide.

Potala Palace

Located in Tibet, this palace served as the winter residence of the Dalai Lama.

It is known for its grand architecture and spiritual significance.

Ha Long Bay

A UNESCO World Heritage site in Vietnam, famous for its limestone karsts.

It is a popular destination for tourists seeking scenic boat tours.
